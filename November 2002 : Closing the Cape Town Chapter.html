November 2002 : Closing the Cape Town Chapter
Greetings and salutations

The last few months have been quite an exciting time for Wendy and I. Our preparations for our overseas trip next year are nearing completion (although there always seems to be just one more thing that needs to be done), our plane tickets have been bought and confirmed, and we depart South Africa’s shores on 3rd Feb for destinations unknown.

Our travel plans seem to fluctuate daily, dictated by discoveries in our Lonely Planet guide books, fluctuations in the exchange rate, and thus our budget, and surprisingly enough the availability of flights. However, this was always our intention – we are trying to commit to as few fixed dates as possible, so we can follow our noses wherever our fancy takes us.

We leave Cape Town just before Christmas, and head down to Plett to visit the Crawford Brunts for Christmas and New Year. We’re looking forward to swimming in a sea that doesn’t instantly turn your toes into ice blocks – 5 years in Cape Town and we still aren’t used to the water.

After New Year we will be heading up the east coast, stopping to visit family and friends along the way, and eventually ending up in Nelspruit for Wendy’s Mum’s birthday. Then, its back to Plett, were we sell our car to my parents – Ian, having reached the learner driver stage will hopefully have as much fun driving it as I have. (And, we hope, leave fewer dents than me!)

On the bus, we head back to Cape Town to catch the plane to our first destination – Kuala Lumpur in Malaysia. We have a triangular ticket that takes us from Cape Town to KL (getting into the traveller’s lingo), to London and then back to Cape Town, which is valid for one year.

From KL, we plan to make our way up to the northern parts of Thailand, and then make our way down the country by train and bus, visiting temples, palaces, working elephants, beaches and cities along the way, until we arrive back at the northern Malaysia border a few weeks later.

Then its off to the island of Panang, apparently once the first British settlement in South East Asia, before making our way via train down to Singapore where we plan to oggle at the shopping malls, visit the zoo and generally feel poor.

From Singapore, we are hoping to find an amazing flight deal to get us down to Dempasar in Bali, where, temperature permitting, we are considering hiring bicycles to tour the island. Otherwise, we will do the more conventional thing, and tour by Bemo (minibus taxi).

During the next month or two we will slowly make our way north-west through Indonesia, stopping at beaches, volcanoes, lakes, temples, palaces and an oranatang sanctuary along the way, until our South East Asia budget is reached. (Which could be longer than we had planned, given how the Rand is strengthening against the dollar!)

A short ferry trip will get us back to KL, where we fly to London for the English summer (something of a change to the 34 degree average in Indonesia) for six months of contract work to pay for the second part of our trip. This, we hope, will be a westwards trip around the world, stopping in Ecuador, Peru and Bolivia in South America, before continuing to New Zealand and Australia, and then back to Cape Town (via Zanzibar if we can convince an airline to take us there) in time for Christmas 2003.

The last time we wrote, we were hoping to land jobs on a cruise liner to help pay for the second part of our trip. However, the millions of CVs we sent to the cruise liner companies seemed to go largely unheeded. It seems our skills in Finance and IT are more in demand back on dry land – plus, its about time we started using our British passports.

Speaking of jobs – if you haven’t already heard, Vardus, the Internet Consulting company where I worked was liquidated at the end of September this year. Rather than being a bad thing, it was actually a fantastic opportunity for a colleague and I to start our own company, continuing the relationship with the ex-Vardus clients that we had built up over the previous two years.

Applicationjunction.com has been going strong for three months now, and it looks like we will be growing from strength to strength in the coming year. Our first employee joins us mid-December, to fill my shoes as Wendy and I gallivant around the globe. While I will be less involved in the day to day running of the business, I am taking my laptop with us on our travels, and I hope to be able to continue to develop products that the business can sell. If you haven’t already, have a look at our webpage – http://www.applicationjunction.com. And, should you be looking for someone who specialises in taking ideas for computer applications and turning them into working products, you know where to turn.

Wendy and I are also in the process of revamping and relaunching our own website, that, unsurprisingly, will be geared towards our travel stories and pictures. We have recently acquired a digital camera that really is one of the marvels of modern technology. Not only can it take up to 500 ordinary photographs (which can be developed on ordinary film), but it also takes movies, can act as a webcamera, and of course, connects to our computer so we can email pictures with great ease. The best feature however, is the fact that you can see your picture as soon as it is taken, decide if it is good enough and then either keep it or erase it and take another.

We are planning on writing a weekly or fortnightly email newsletter during our travels; full of the best stories and pictures from the preceding days. The newsletter will be mailed by our website – where you will be able to subscribe and unsubscribe, view previous newsletters and see extra pictures of our travels.

We plan to launch the website early in January, along with our first newsletter. The URL will be: http://www.castlelaing.com and our email addresses david@castlelaing.com & wendy@castlelaing.com or tea@castlelaing.com to reach us both.

That’s all the news for now – more exciting stories to come soon!
